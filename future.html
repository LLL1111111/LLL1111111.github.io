01 / infrastructure

AI Infrastructure and Inference

Inference cost, memory bandwidth, networking, storage, and power are becoming the practical boundary of what products can ship.

Signals

  • Cluster interconnect becomes product-critical
  • Inference and long-context workloads change memory pressure
  • High availability matters as training spans more clusters and data centres

Tracking

  • Where inference cost actually goes
  • How GPU/CPU/network coupling changes system design
  • Which bottleneck moves next: memory, network, power, or reliability
02 / agents

Agent Systems and Context Engineering

As agents take on longer tasks, context construction, editing, persistence, and evaluation become a core software layer.

Signals

  • Agent trajectory data becomes a training asset
  • Context engineering turns into an industry-wide stack
  • Agentic workloads need async execution and tool-rich environments

Tracking

  • Agent architectures that survive long-horizon work
  • Evaluation loops for memory-rich agents
  • How agent workloads reshape compute scheduling

Related post: Context Engineering: An Industry-Wide Technical Landscape.

03 / multimodal

Multimodal and World Models

Video, 3D reconstruction, world models, and language agents are converging into broader systems for perception, planning, and action.

Signals

  • World models become interaction environments
  • Multimodal UI moves AI closer to everyday products
  • Embodied intelligence raises the bar for grounding

Tracking

  • Whether progress is compute-bound or data-bound
  • How grounding changes agent design
  • Workload patterns in multimodal models
04 / product

AI Product Strategy

Technical constraints in infrastructure and agents eventually become product roadmaps, pricing, distribution, and defensibility.

Signals

  • AI workflows become default software behavior
  • Agent products need state, tools, and persistent context
  • Consumer AI hardware tests whether interaction can become ambient

Tracking

  • Where durable product advantages come from
  • How cost changes product design
  • What the next interface layer looks like

Signals

  • AI capex concentration
  • Power, networking, and reliability constraints
  • Photonic networking and European compute projects become watch items

Tracking

  • Where consensus misprices bottlenecks
  • How technical limits affect valuation
  • Which second-order beneficiaries matter
